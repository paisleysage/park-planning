Hotels & Transportation

There are 3 types of on-property hotels and resorts at Walt Disney World. These categories are Value, Moderate, and Deluxe. All on property resorts have a quick-service resturant, a gift shop, free and paid activities, pools, early entry to the parks, and free transportation and parking at the resorts. There are a few types of transportation, depending on the resort. When choosing your resort it is important to take into account the location of the resort, the transportation options, ammenities, and your budget.

Value Resorts

Value Resorts are going to be the chapest options, usually between $130 and $440 per night depending on the time of year, availability, and room type. They are usually farthest from the parks, with limited options for dining and entertainment, as well as limited transportation options. The theming very Disney, with large statues of Disney characters and bright colors.

Moderate Resorts

Moderate Resorts are going to be the the mid range price wise, usually between $330 and $600 per night depending on the time of year, availability, and room type. They will usually be larger resorts with internal busses, meaning you may have to take a bus to get to the loby, dining, and transportation to the other parks, depending on the room you get. They usually have a few more options for transportation to the parks, as well as more entertainment and dining options. The theming is more subtly Disney.

Deluxe Resorts

Deluxe Resorts are going to be the most expensive options, usually between $440 and $1000 per night depending on the time of year, availability, and room type. They often have multiple dining options, including character dining. They also are closest to the parks, with faster transportation. A few have spas and nicer pools. The theming only has a hint of Disney, with luxerious decor.

Resort Hopping

When planning a trip to Disney World, most people just think about the parks. But the resorts have so much to offer! Planning a day to explore the resorts is a great idea and is so fun! There is no transportation between resorts, so if you are trying to get to a resort, you need to fist take a bus to a park, the TTC, or Disney Springs

Monorail Loop

  1. Take a bus to Magic Kingdom
  2. Get on the Resort Monorail
  3. Take the monorail to Contemporary Resort
  4. Take the monorail to Polynesian Resort
  5. Take the monorail to Grand Floridian Resort
  6. Take the monorail to Magic Kingdom
  7. Take a bus home

Skyliner Loop

  1. Take a bus to Hollywood Studios
  2. Take the skyliner to Art of Animation Resort
  3. Walk to Pop Century Resort
  4. Take the skyliner to Carribean Beach Resort
  5. Take the skyliner to the Riviera
  6. Take the skyliner back to Hollywood Studios
  7. Take a bus home
